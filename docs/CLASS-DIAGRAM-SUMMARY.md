# Find It – Class Diagram Summary

The **Find It Class Diagram** models the main domain entities and their relationships. It is organised into logical groups: **Location** (Province → District → City hierarchy); **Merchant / Outlet** (Merchant with one-to-many SubMerchants, both linked to Outlets; Outlet has Payments and OutletSchedules and is tied to Province, District, and City); **Customer** (Customer with CustomerFavorites and Feedback, both associated with Outlet in many-to-many or one-to-many ways); **Catalog** (Category and Item, with Item belonging to a Category and an Outlet); **Discount** (Discount and DiscountItem linking discounts to Items); and a standalone **User** entity (with role, and optional merchantId, subMerchantId, and customerId for linking to the respective actors). The diagram uses thick blue association lines with multiplicities (e.g. 1–*, *–1, *–*) to show cardinalities and keeps the layout in columns to reduce line crossings, focusing on entity-level structure rather than inheritance or operations.
